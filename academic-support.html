Advising & Study Help

Learn how to use the Erskine course catalog, plan your schedule, and connect with academic support resources.

How to Use the Erskine Course Catalog

  1. Start with your core requirements (general education).
  2. Find your major requirements and note prerequisites.
  3. Check which classes are offered in the current term schedule.
  4. Build a draft schedule that keeps you at or above full-time status.
  5. Review your choices with your advisor or the Peer Navigator.

Planning Your Schedule for Different Student Paths

Regular Student

  • Take at least 12 credit hours to stay full-time.
  • Balance core classes with major requirements.
  • Aim for a mix of reading-heavy and problem-solving courses.
  • Leave time for work, activities, and rest.

Student-Athlete (NCAA)

  • Stay at or above full-time credit load.
  • Coordinate with your coach and compliance to make sure you meet NCAA progress-toward-degree rules.
  • Schedule practice and travel days before you pick late labs or night classes.
  • Use study halls, SI, and labs to keep grades strong in-season.

Honors Student

  • Make sure you enroll in the correct Honors seminars each term.
  • Plan ahead for larger writing and reading loads.
  • Pair Honors courses with at least one ‚Äúlighter‚Äù class when possible.
  • Protect blocks of time for reading, drafts, and revisions.
